Axolotl
The axolotl is a paedomorphic salamander closely related to the tiger salamander. It is unusual among amphibians in that it reaches adulthood without undergoing metamorphosis. Instead of taking to the land, adults remain aquatic and gilled.
Information About Axolotls

The name "axolotl" comes from the Aztec god Xolotl, who could transform into an axolotl to escape his enemies.

Axolotls are small salamanders with feathery gills on the outside of their bodies and come in a variety of colors, including brown, pink, and green. They can also change color to camouflage themselves.

In the wild, axolotls can only be found in Lake Xochimilco in Mexico City. However, they are critically endangered due to habitat destruction, invasive species, water pollution, and an amphibian fungus.

Axolotls can regrow appendages that are cut off or damaged. Doctors are studying how axolotls regenerate body parts in the hopes of developing technology that could help humans.
